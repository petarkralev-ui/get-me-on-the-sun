Flying In

Getting to the island

✈️

Direct flights from major hubs

From most large European cities, you can fly direct — no need to route through Madrid or another connecting city. Flights run very frequently, multiple times a day from many origin cities, year-round rather than just in peak season.

💶

Budget carriers cover most routes

Ryanair, easyJet, and Jet2 all serve Tenerife well, making regular trips back and forth genuinely affordable — useful whether you're visiting during the buying process or flying back to check on the property later.

🛬

Tenerife South (Reina Sofia) is the one that matters

The island has two airports. Tenerife South is closer to the main southern resort areas — where most non-resident buyers end up purchasing — so it's usually the more relevant airport when you're weighing travel time against a specific property.

On The Island

Getting around once you land

🚗

Renting a car is essential

Public transport exists, but if you're viewing properties, meeting agents, or handling paperwork across different parts of the island, a rental car is essential rather than optional. It's the difference between a productive trip and losing half your day to buses and timing.
