Once the property is yours, the two things that matter most are turning it into income, and making sure it doesn't become a problem when you're not there to see it.

Income

Renting it out

🏝️

Short-term holiday rental works best

For income, a short-term, Airbnb-style holiday rental tends to outperform a long-term tenant arrangement — Tenerife's year-round tourism means occupancy holds up outside the traditional summer season too, which a long-term let can't capture.

🗂️

Use a local property management company

Handling bookings, cleaning, check-ins, and guest issues remotely is close to impossible to do well from abroad. A local property management company that handles this end-to-end is what makes short-term renting realistic when you're not living on the island full-time.

Protection

Keeping small problems small

🔧

A trusted local maintenance contact is essential

The single biggest risk for an owner who isn't on the island full-time is a small maintenance issue going unnoticed until it becomes an expensive one. A trusted, local, on-call maintenance contact — someone who can check on the property and act quickly — is essential, not optional. This is what actually protects the value of the property between your visits.
