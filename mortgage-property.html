What We Handle First

The foundation that keeps the whole purchase moving

🪪

We book and manage your NIE appointment early

The NIE (your foreigner ID number) is required for almost every step that follows — the mortgage, the notary, even opening a bank account. The paperwork side of this was, by far, the biggest source of delay in the whole process. The most reliable route is applying in person at a police station in Spain, rather than going through a consulate abroad, where wait times can add weeks. We can also book your appointment for you and arrange for a Spanish guide and translator to attend with you, so no time is wasted. We keep up with all the documents needed too, so you don't have to.

🤝

We package your mortgage file properly

Spanish mortgage bureaucracy is hard to manage alone from abroad, especially without fluent Spanish. We coordinate with people who already work with non-resident buyers, understand which banks are realistic, know what documents are needed, and package your file so it does not stall halfway through.

🏦

We keep the bank process moving

A strong bank-side contact can make a real difference to approval speed, document checks, and the repayment rate you are offered. We help keep that communication clear, make sure your file stays complete, and push the process forward before small gaps become long delays.

📊

We build the property search around real lending numbers

As a non-resident, Spanish banks typically finance somewhere in the 60–70% range of the property's value — not the higher percentages sometimes available to residents. We shape your property search and deposit planning around those numbers from the start, so the deal still works when the bank valuation arrives.

📝

We help protect you before the pre-agreement is signed

The pre-agreement is where the deal conditions need to be clear, especially around deposits, valuation, financing, deadlines, and what happens if something changes. We help with the negotiation and wording so you understand exactly what you are agreeing to, there are no hidden risks in the documents, and the seller cannot slip in cheeky terms that work against you later.

🌐

We stay with you through the Spanish-speaking steps

If you do not speak Spanish, that should not stop the purchase. We arrange English- and Russian-speaking support where needed, from the first enquiry and property viewing through bank meetings, police appointments, contract checks, and signing at the notary.

The risk we check before you sign

The real risk isn't the mortgage — it's the contract with the seller's agency. When you agree a price and sign with an agency, the contract terms determine what happens if the bank's official valuation later comes in below that price.

Some contracts are written in the buyer's favor and protect your deposit if this happens. Others aren't — and if the valuation falls short and your contract doesn't cover you, you can lose your deposit entirely, which can run into the thousands.

Our role: we help you check the valuation risk, bring in independent support where needed, and make sure the purchase contract has the right protection clauses before you commit your money.
